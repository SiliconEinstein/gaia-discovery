### Novel Mechanism: Gap Autocorrelation and the Selberg Integral

The key new object is the **gap autocorrelation function**:

**Why this matters:** If R(1) < 0, then exceptionally small gaps (d_n ≪ 1) tend to be followed by large gaps (d_{n+1} ≫ 1). This creates an **energy barrier** against sequences of small gaps.

**Step 1:** For the minimum gap to approach 0, we need windows [n, n+M] where multiple gaps are small. Otherwise isolated small gaps don't affect lim inf.

**Step 2:** The energy functional for a window is:
E[{d_i}_{i=n}^{n+M}] = Σ_{i=n}^{n+M-1} (d_i - 1)² + 2λ·Σ_{i=n}^{n+M-1} (d_i - 1)(d_{i+1} - 1)

where λ = -R(1) > 0 is the anticorrelation strength.

**Step 3:** Under the constraint Σ d_i = M (mean spacing preserved), minimizing the first gap d_n subject to energy E ≤ E_0 gives:

d_min ≥ 1 - √(E_0/(1 + λ))/M

This bounds the total energy: E[{d_i}] ≲ T.

**Step 5:** For a window of size M ~ log T (natural correlation length), we get:
E_0 ~ log T

Therefore:
d_min ≥ 1 - √(log T)/[(1 + λ)·log T] = 1 - 1/√[(1 + λ)·log T]

**Step 6:** Converting back to the original scaling:
lim inf d_n ≥ 1 - 1/√[(1 + λ)·log γ_n] → 1 as n → ∞ if λ > 0

This contradicts μ = 0!
