## Understanding the Route Structure

The target statement asks about analyzing the statistical properties of consecutive gaps between Riemann zeta zeros on the critical line, specifically whether there exists μ < 1 such that lim inf d_n < μ, and what the optimal constant is.

The route proposes:
1. Starting from established facts (RH, zero counting, Goldston's S'(t) formula)
2. Introducing a **new object**: N₂(μ, T) counting consecutive small gap pairs
3. Using Cauchy-Schwarz with the S-function energy budget to derive a bound
4. Concluding a new theorem about consecutive small gaps

## Key Propositions to Extract

**Seeds (established facts):**
- RH assumption
- Zero counting function asymptotics
- Normalized gap definition and average = 1
- Goldston's S'(t) second moment formula
- Current best result: lim inf d_n < 0.50895 under RH
- The d_n + ΔS_n = 1 + O(1/log γ_n) relation

**New Objects/Bridges (route innovations):**
- Definition of N₂(μ, T) - consecutive small gap pair count
- The Cauchy-Schwarz mechanism for bounding S' over consecutive small gaps
- The disjoint extraction argument (factor of 3)
- The final bound N₂(μ,T) ≤ μ/(2(1-μ)²) · M

**Experimental/Verifiable Claims:**
- GUE predicts ~2% for consecutive pairs with d_n, d_{n+1} < 0.4
- Odlyzko data should confirm the bound

**Risks/Gaps:**
- Error term propagation from O(1/log γ_n) 
- The bound is far weaker than GUE predictions (5/9 vs ~0.02)
- This doesn't directly improve μ = 0.50895

- **B**: Structural bridges like the consecutive pair definition, the S-function increment relation
