# act_ff109e42c026 — Support Evidence for HEK293T as Lentivirus Packaging Cell Line

## Claim
HEK293T (or HEK293FT) cells are the standard cell line for producing lentiviral particles used in shRNA delivery experiments. They express SV40 large T antigen, which enhances plasmid replication from vectors with SV40 origins (e.g., psPAX2, pMD2.G), yielding high-titer lentivirus.

## Evidence Summary

### Mechanism
HEK293T is a derivative of the HEK293 human embryonic kidney cell line that stably expresses the SV40 large T antigen. This antigen binds to and promotes replication of plasmids containing the SV40 origin of replication (ori). Most lentiviral packaging plasmids (second-generation psPAX2 and third-generation pMDLg/pRRE + pRSV-Rev) and the VSV-G envelope plasmid (pMD2.G) carry SV40 ori. Co-transfection into HEK293T cells therefore results in:
1. Higher plasmid copy number per cell via SV40 ori-driven episomal replication
2. Increased expression of packaging proteins (Gag, Pol, Rev) and envelope glycoprotein (VSV-G)
3. Higher viral titers (typically 10^6–10^8 TU/mL) compared to parental HEK293 or other lines

### Key Literature Evidence

1. **Naldini et al. 1996** (Science 272:263-267, cited 4,896 times). Seminal paper developing the HIV-based lentiviral vector system. Used 293T cells for vector production. Established the lentivirus platform.

2. **Dull et al. 1998** (J Virol 72:8463-8471, cited 3,362 times). "A Third-Generation Lentivirus Vector with a Conditional Packaging System." The foundational paper for the safety-improved packaging system now standard in shRNA experiments. Uses 293T cells. Abstract confirms: achieved titers up to 10^7 TU/mL and 10^4 TU/ng p24.

3. **Zufferey et al. 1997** (Nat Biotechnol 15:871-875, cited 2,023 times). "Multiply attenuated lentiviral vector achieves efficient gene delivery in vivo." Key paper on safety improvements using separate packaging plasmids, establishing the split-plasmid approach used today with 293T cells.

4. **Stewart et al. 2003** (RNA 9:493-501, cited 1,637 times). "Lentivirus-delivered stable gene silencing by RNAi in primary cells." First paper demonstrating stable shRNA-mediated gene silencing via lentiviral delivery in primary cells. Used 293T cells. Directly relevant to the problem context (shRNA + lentivirus).

5. **Tiscornia et al. 2006** (Nat Protoc 1:241-245, cited 1,029 times). "Production and purification of lentiviral vectors." The canonical Nature Protocols paper for lentivirus production. A widely cited standard protocol that uses HEK293T cells.

6. **Campeau et al. 2009** (PLoS ONE 4:e6529, cited 1,033 times). "A Versatile Viral System for Expression and Depletion of Proteins in Mammalian Cells." Describes a comprehensive Gateway-compatible lentiviral vector system for cDNA/shRNA/miRNA expression. Uses 293T extensively. This is one of the most widely used lentiviral shRNA systems.

7. **Merten et al. 2016** (Mol Ther Methods Clin Dev 3:16017, cited 282 times). "Production of lentiviral vectors." Review that explicitly states: "clinical-grade LV are in most of the cases produced by transient transfection of 293 or 293T cells grown in cell factories." This review directly confirms 293T as the standard production cell line.

8. **Cockrell & Kafri 2007** (Mol Biotechnol 36:184-204, cited 360 times). "Gene delivery by lentivirus vectors." Comprehensive review of lentivirus gene delivery methodology.

9. **Jiang et al. 2015** (Sci Rep 5:13875, cited 156 times). "An optimized method for high-titer lentivirus preparations without ultracentrifugation." Protocol for high-titer lentivirus using 293T cells (up to 2x10^8 TU/mL).

10. **Bulcha et al. 2021** (Signal Transduct Target Ther 6:53, cited 1,276 times). "Viral vector platforms within the gene therapy landscape." Broad review covering AAV, adenovirus, and lentivirus vectors, confirming 293T as the production platform.

11. **Addgene Lentivirus Production Protocol** (addgene.org). Addgene, the major plasmid repository, provides a standard lentivirus production protocol using HEK293T/HEK293FT cells with PEI or calcium phosphate transfection.

### HEK293T vs HEK293FT
HEK293FT (Invitrogen/Thermo Fisher) is a fast-growing variant of HEK293T, also expressing SV40 large T antigen. Both are interchangeable for lentivirus production. HEK293FT is specifically marketed for lentivirus production (ViraPower Lentiviral System).

### Counter-Evidence and Limitations

1. **Suspension-adapted 293T lines** are increasingly used for large-scale production in bioreactors. However, these are variants of the same 293T cell line.
2. **Stable producer cell lines** (e.g., based on 293, HT1080, or CHO cells) exist for clinical/commercial GMP production. However, for research-scale shRNA experiments, transient transfection of 293T remains the dominant approach.
3. **VSV-G pseudotyping** (not the cell line per se) confers the broad tropism that makes lentivirus useful. The cell line choice is about production efficiency, not vector properties.

### Conclusion
The evidence is overwhelming and unambiguous: HEK293T (or its derivative HEK293FT) is the standard cell line for lentivirus production in shRNA experiments. This is established by:
- The entire foundational literature on lentivirus vectors since 1996
- Explicit statements in review articles (Merten et al. 2016)
- Standard protocols in Nature Protocols, Current Protocols, and Addgene
- The biological rationale (SV40 large T antigen enhancing plasmid replication)
- Thousands of published studies using this cell line for lentivirus production

**Confidence**: Very high (0.95+). This is molecular biology textbook knowledge with no credible countervailing evidence.
