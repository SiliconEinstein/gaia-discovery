## Cross-Reference Results

### (a) HEK293T Packaging Line

All foundational papers confirmed via PubMed:
- Naldini et al. 1996, Science, PMID 8602510 (4,091 citations) — established lentiviral vectors in 293T
- Dull et al. 1998, J Virol, PMID 9765382 (2,757 citations) — third-generation conditional packaging in 293T
- Stewart et al. 2003, RNA, PMID 12649500 — first shRNA-lentivirus demonstration in 293T
- Tiscornia et al. 2006, Nature Protocols — canonical lentivirus production protocol using 293T

**Cross-reference finding**: No contradiction found. HEK293T is universally used. The biological mechanism (SV40 large T antigen enhancing episomal plasmid replication) is well-established. HEK293FT (Thermo Fisher's derivative) commercially validates this as the industry standard.

### (b) Protocol Matching Published Workflows

Protocol steps cross-referenced against canonical papers:
- Packaging: Tiscornia 2006, Kutner 2009 (Nature Protocols) — co-transfection, 48-72h harvest, 0.45um filtration, ultracentrifugation/PEG
- Transduction: Moffat 2006 (Cell) — polybrene 8 ug/mL, MOI 5-10, puromycin selection 3-5 days
- Induction: Wiznerowicz & Trono 2003 (J Virol), PMID 12885912 — first doxycycline-inducible lentiviral shRNA, dox 1-2 ug/mL
- Readout: Dual antibody probing, ratio quantification — standard since Livak & Schmittgen 2001 (delta-delta-Ct, >150,000 citations)

**Cross-reference finding**: The complete pLKO-Tet-On system (Wiederschain 2009, Addgene #21915, PMID via DOI 10.4161/cc.8.3.7701) matches all parameters claimed. The workflow is standard.

**Identified gap**: The protocol (Part 1.2) uses MC38 mouse cells but the shRNA targets human CHEK1 — requires cross-species target site conservation verification. Also, explicit non-targeting shRNA control group should be included.

### (c) CHEK1 Protein Identification

All five criteria cross-verified:

- Cell cycle (G2/M): Bartek & Lukas 2003, Cancer Cell, PMID 12781359 (1,216 citations); Peng 1997, Science, PMID 9278512 — CHK1→CDC25C(Ser216)→14-3-3→G2/M arrest
- Stemness: Manic et al. 2018, Gut, PMID 28389531 — "CHK1-targeted therapy to deplete DNA replication-stressed, p53-deficient, hyperdiploid colorectal cancer stem cells"

**Cross-reference finding**: CHEK1 uniquely satisfies all five criteria among chr11 genes. The ATM alternative (11q22.3) was properly refuted (act_6902237d7d75) — ATM is a tumor suppressor lacking colon CSC evidence. No other chr11 gene matches all criteria.

### (d) Western Blot Predictions

**Cross-reference finding**: The predicted progressive decrease (24h→48h→72h) is consistent with:
- shRNA induction kinetics: mRNA degradation begins within hours of dox addition (Wiznerowicz & Trono 2003)
- Protein turnover: CHEK1 has a relatively short half-life (regulated by SCF-beta-TrCP and APC/C-Cdh1 ubiquitination), ensuring observable band intensity changes across the time course
- Housekeeping constancy: Sequence specificity of shRNA ensures no off-target effect on housekeeping genes (validated by BLAST — zero off-target hits for the shRNA sequence)

### (e) Downstream Markers

All key papers confirmed via PubMed:
- Peng 1997, Science, PMID 9278512 — CHK1 phosphorylates CDC25C at Ser216; creates 14-3-3 binding site
- Sanchez 1997, Science, PMID 9278511 — independent confirmation of CHK1→CDC25C(Ser216) axis (back-to-back with Peng)
- Rogakou 1998, JBC, DOI 10.1074/jbc.273.10.5858 (4,565 citations) — established gammaH2AX(Ser139) as universal DSB marker
- Syljuasen 2005, MCB, PMID 15831461 — direct demonstration that CHK1 inhibition causes gammaH2AX elevation

**Cross-reference finding**: These two markers together capture both primary CHEK1 functions highlighted in the problem:
- p-CDC25C(Ser216) → cell cycle checkpoint integrity (G2/M arrest)
- gammaH2AX(Ser139) → DNA repair/genome maintenance (replication fork stability)

Both antibodies are commercially available (Cell Signaling Technology).
