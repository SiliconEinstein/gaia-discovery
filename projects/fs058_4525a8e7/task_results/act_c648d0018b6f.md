## Candidate Evaluation

### Candidate 1: CHEK1 (Checkpoint Kinase 1) — 11q24.2

**Chromosomal location:** Verified at 11q24.2 via NCBI Gene ID 1111.

**DNA repair function:**
- NCBI Gene summary: "required for checkpoint mediated cell cycle arrest in response to DNA damage or the presence of unreplicated DNA"
- Key role in ATR-CHK1 pathway: regulates replication fork stability and homologous recombination repair
- Manicardi et al. (2021, iScience): "CHK1 drives the DNA damage repair of CR-CSphCs" (colorectal cancer sphere cells)
- Integrates signals from ATM and ATR to coordinate DNA damage response

**Cell cycle regulation:**
- NCBI: "Phosphorylation of CDC25A protein phosphatase by this protein is required for cells to delay cell cycle progression in response to double-strand DNA breaks"
- Central G2/M checkpoint kinase
- Also regulates S-phase checkpoint and intra-S checkpoint

**Stemness function:**
- Manic et al. (2017, Gut, 101 citations): "CHK1-targeted therapy to deplete DNA replication-stressed, p53-deficient, hyperdiploid colorectal cancer stem cells" — CRC stem cells are "exquisitely dependent on CHK1 function"
- Manicardi et al. (2021, iScience, 75 citations): "CHK1 inhibitor sensitizes resistant colorectal cancer stem cells to nortopsentin" — CHK1 drives DNA repair of colorectal cancer stem cells
- Wang et al. (2012, Cancer Research, 228 citations): "MYC Regulation of CHK1 and CHK2 Promotes Radioresistance in a Stem Cell-like Population of Nasopharyngeal Carcinoma Cells" — MYC transcriptionally activates CHK1/CHK2 in cancer stem-like cells

**Colon cancer context:**
- Gali-Muhtasib et al. (2008, Cancer Research, 178 citations): "Thymoquinone Triggers Inactivation of the Stress Response Pathway Sensor CHEK1 and Contributes to Apoptosis in Colorectal Cancer Cells"
- Both Manic (2017) and Manicardi (2021) studies are in colorectal cancer models
- Frequently overexpressed in colorectal cancer, associated with poor prognosis and chemoresistance

**shRNA knockdown viability:**
- CHEK1 is commonly targeted by shRNA/siRNA in published cancer studies
- Its pro-survival function makes it a logical knockdown target to study loss-of-function effects in cancer cells

### Candidate 2: ATM (Ataxia Telangiectasia Mutated) — 11q22.3

**Chromosomal location:** Verified at 11q22.3 via NCBI Gene ID 472.

**DNA repair function:** Master regulator of DNA double-strand break repair; phosphorylates numerous downstream effectors including p53, BRCA1, CHK2, NBS1.

**Cell cycle regulation:** Activates G1/S, intra-S, and G2/M cell cycle checkpoints via p53 and CHK2 pathways.

**Stemness function:**
- Role primarily established in hematopoietic stem cell (HSC) maintenance
- No direct evidence found for ATM specifically regulating colorectal cancer stem cell populations
- Literature searches for "ATM + colorectal cancer + stem cell" returned only general review articles, not primary research demonstrating ATM's role in colon CSCs

**Colon cancer context:**
- ATM mutations are found in a subset of colorectal cancers (TCGA data)
- However, ATM is predominantly studied as a **tumor suppressor** — loss-of-function mutations predispose to cancer
- ATM knockdown experiments are less typical in cancer biology because knocking down a tumor suppressor is not the standard approach to study its role in oncogenesis

**Key limitations for matching the problem statement:**
- The problem describes a protein that is "highly expressed in colon cancer" and knocked down by shRNA. ATM is typically a tumor suppressor (loss-of-function context), not a protein that is overexpressed and targeted for therapeutic knockdown in cancer.
- ATM's connection to "stemness" specifically in colon cancer is much weaker than CHEK1's.
- No literature evidence found for ATM knockdown experiments in colon cancer stem cells.
