### Criterion 2: DNA Repair Function

**Sorensen et al. (2005)**, Nat Cell Biol 7(2):195-201. PMID 15665856. DOI: 10.1038/ncb1212
- Title: "The cell-cycle checkpoint kinase Chk1 is required for mammalian homologous recombination repair"
- Seminal paper directly establishing CHK1 as essential for homologous recombination (HR) repair in mammalian cells. Chk1-deficient cells show defective HR repair and persistent DNA damage.

**Smith et al. (2010)**, Adv Cancer Res 108:73-112. PMID 21034966. DOI: 10.1016/B978-0-12-380888-2.00003-0
- Title: "The ATM-Chk2 and ATR-Chk1 pathways in DNA damage signaling and cancer"
- Authoritative review positioning CHK1 as the central effector kinase of the ATR-CHK1 DNA damage response pathway, regulating replication fork stability and DNA repair.

**Gonzalez Besteiro & Gottifredi (2015)**, Mutat Res Rev Mutat Res 763:246-257. PMID 25795119. DOI: 10.1016/j.mrrev.2014.10.003
- Title: "The fork and the kinase: a DNA replication tale from a CHK1 perspective"
- Review focused on CHK1's role in stabilizing stalled replication forks, preventing fork collapse, and coordinating replication with DNA repair.

**Smits & Gillespie (2015)**, FEBS J 282(19):3681-3692. PMID 26216057. DOI: 10.1111/febs.13387
- Title: "DNA damage control: regulation and functions of checkpoint kinase 1"
- Comprehensive review of CHK1 functions in DNA damage response, including DNA repair coordination and replication fork protection.

### Criterion 3: Cell Cycle Regulation

**Bartek & Lukas (2003)**, Cancer Cell 3(5):421-429. PMID 12781359. DOI: 10.1016/s1535-6108(03)00110-7
- Title: "Chk1 and Chk2 kinases in checkpoint control and cancer"
- Landmark review establishing CHK1 as a central G2/M checkpoint kinase that phosphorylates CDC25 phosphatases, preventing CDK1/cyclin B activation and enforcing cell cycle arrest.

**Bulavin et al. (2002)**, Curr Opin Genet Dev 12(1):92-97. PMID 11790561. DOI: 10.1016/s0959-437x(01)00270-2
- Title: "p38 and Chk1 kinases: different conductors for the G2/M checkpoint symphony"
- Review describing CHK1's role as a G2/M checkpoint kinase.

**Chen & Sanchez (2004)**, DNA Repair (Amst) 3(8-9):1025-1032. PMID 15279789. DOI: 10.1016/j.dnarep.2004.03.003
- Title: "Chk1 in the DNA damage response: conserved roles from yeasts to mammals"
- Review covering CHK1's conserved role in G2/M checkpoint arrest through CDC25C phosphorylation, from yeast to human.

**CHK1-CDC25C Mechanism** (well-established): CHK1 directly phosphorylates CDC25C at Ser216, creating a 14-3-3 binding site that sequesters CDC25C in the cytoplasm, preventing CDK1/cyclin B activation and enforcing G2/M arrest. Also phosphorylates CDC25A (targeting it for ubiquitin-mediated degradation) to enforce intra-S and G1/S checkpoints.

### Criterion 4: Stemness Maintenance

**Manic et al. (2018)**, Gut 67(5):903-917. PMID 28389531. DOI: 10.1136/gutjnl-2016-312623
- Title: "CHK1-targeted therapy to deplete DNA replication-stressed, p53-deficient, hyperdiploid colorectal cancer stem cells"
- **Directly demonstrates** that CHK1 inhibition with LY2606368 (prexasertib) depletes colorectal cancer stem cells (CSCs). Shows that CHK1 is essential for maintaining CRC stem cell viability through replication stress management. p53-deficient, hyperdiploid CRC stem cells are particularly dependent on CHK1.

**Ronco et al. (2017)**, MedChemComm 8(2):295-319. PMID 30108746. DOI: 10.1039/c6md00439c
- Title: "ATM, ATR, CHK1, CHK2 and WEE1 inhibitors in cancer and cancer stem cells"
- Review covering the role of DNA damage checkpoint kinases including CHK1 in cancer stem cell maintenance and the therapeutic potential of CHK1 inhibitors against CSCs.

### Criterion 5: Colon Cancer Relevance

**Wang et al. (2017)**, Biomed Res Int 2017:5718968. PMID 29082250. DOI: 10.1155/2017/5718968
- Title: "XRRA1 Targets ATM/CHK1/2-Mediated DNA Repair in Colorectal Cancer"
- Confirms CHK1 involvement in DNA repair pathways specifically in colorectal cancer context.

### Additional Supporting Context

- CHK1 is a recognized therapeutic target in oncology; CHK1 inhibitors (prexasertib/LY2606368) are in clinical trials for various cancers including colorectal cancer.
- CHEK1 is one of very few genes on chromosome 11 that simultaneously functions in DNA repair, cell cycle checkpoint regulation, and cancer stem cell maintenance.
