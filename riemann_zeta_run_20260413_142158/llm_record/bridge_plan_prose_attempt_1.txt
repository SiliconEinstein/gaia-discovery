## Structure Overview

The route proposes a novel approach to improving the small gap bound for Riemann zeta zeros. The key innovation is the **Wirtinger-Repulsion Bound** — a claimed unconditional-under-RH result that quantifies level repulsion without requiring Montgomery's full conjecture. This is then combined with modified Beurling-Selberg minorants to potentially push the bound below 0.50895.

The structure breaks into:

1. **Established seeds**: RH, zero counting, Montgomery partial results, current best bound 0.50895, GUE statistics
2. **Novel constructions**: Wirtinger-Repulsion Bound, Repulsion-Assisted Minorant Principle
3. **Technical derivations**: The optimization problem, specific minorant families
4. **Testable claims**: Numerical verification of repulsion bound, minorant sum computation
5. **Target conjecture**: The final claimed improvement to μ* < 0.50895

## Key Risk Assessment

The **Wirtinger-Repulsion Bound** (P_WRB) is the critical new ingredient. The route provides a proof sketch using Wirtinger inequality + mean-value theorems, but this requires careful verification:
- The Wirtinger application to intervals between zeros needs exact justification
- The summation argument and comparison with moment theorems needs checking
- This is the kind of claim best validated by computation (checking O(ε₀²) scaling on Odlyzko data) before attempting formalization

The **Repulsion-Assisted Minorant Principle** is a structural bridge that's plausible but needs clarification about when negativity near zero is genuinely compensated.

The **specific numerical claim** (μ* ≤ 0.495 with particular parameters) is squarely experimental.
