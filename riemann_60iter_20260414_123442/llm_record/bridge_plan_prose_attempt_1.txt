## Context and Target

The target is to **determine the optimal constant μ** for the normalized gap lim inf under RH. This is an open problem in analytic number theory. The current best rigorous result is μ < 0.50895, but the route attempts to argue that μ > 0 and can be computed explicitly via a connection to GUE statistics.

## Analysis of the Proof Route

The route proposes a **new mechanism** that is not established in the literature:

1. **Core mechanism**: Use variance bounds on ψ(x) oscillations to constrain the count of abnormally small gaps
2. **Bridge hypothesis**: A functional F_T(δ) measuring contributions from close zeros
3. **Key inequality**: V_small(T,δ) ≥ M_T(δ) · T/(log T)² · δ² (this is a new, unproven claim)
4. **Contradiction strategy**: If μ = 0, then too many small gaps would violate the variance bound
5. **Computation formula**: μ defined via a supremum involving gap counts
6. **Final claim**: An explicit formula for μ in terms of the GUE spacing distribution S(s)

- Variance bound V(T) = O(T² log T) under RH

**New constructions** (need bridge/derived/risk grades):

- The variance decomposition into V_small(T,δ) contribution
- The key inequality relating variance, gap count, and δ
- The definition of M_T(δ) as count of small normalized gaps
- The constraint M_T(δ) ≲ T(log T)³/δ² from variance
- The probability formula P(d_n < s) from Montgomery
- The contradiction argument for μ = 0
- The supremum definition of μ
- The balance condition formula
- The explicit computation formula in the conclusion

**Grade A**: The seven seed premises - these are known results or conjectures stated clearly.

**Grade B**: Structural bridges that are mathematically reasonable directions but need more work:
- Defining F_T(δ) and M_T(δ) as counting/measuring objects
- The general idea that variance constrains gap distribution
- The connection between Montgomery's conjecture and gap probabilities

**Grade C**: Claims that should be checked by computation or finite verification before formalization:
- The specific inequality V_small(T,δ) ≥ M_T(δ) · T/(log T)² · δ² with explicit constants
- The constraint M_T(δ) ≲ T(log T)³/δ² derived from it
- The probability formula P(d_n < s) = 1 - exp(-πs²/4) - s√(π/2) exp(-πs²/4)
- The numerical estimate μ ≥ 0.01
- Testing the balance condition on finite data

**Grade D**: Risky or vague claims that are currently too ambiguous:
- The contradiction argument that μ = 0 leads to violation (step 5 is hand-wavy)
- The supremum definition of μ as sup{δ : liminf M_T(δ)/N(T) = 0} - conceptually unclear
- The explicit formula μ = inf{s > 0 : ∫_0^s u·S(u) du · √(log T)/s ≫ 1} - doesn't follow from steps
- The overall conclusion that μ can be explicitly computed

1. Start from the seven seeds
2. Introduce the functional F_T(δ) and counter M_T(δ) as bridge objects
